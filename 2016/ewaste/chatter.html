How Your Old Flatscreen Ends Up In Hong Kong

GPS trackers placed in four electronic devices sent signals from recycling centers in the US to landfills in Hong Kong. Are your old electronics being recycled responsibly? Explore each tacker's journey spanning thousands of miles and hundreds of days. Read "The Circuit" to learn more about ewaste.

Beaverton to Hong Kong

A LCD TV screen is dropped off at an electronics recycling center in Beaverton, Oregon. A GPS tracker has been placed inside the TV. It travels for 299 days and more than 8,700 miles to a scrapyard in Tam Mei, Hong Kong.

Portland to City Fung Kong Tsuen

A LCD TV screen is dropped off at an electronics recycling center in Portland, Oregon. A GPS tracker has been placed inside the TV. It travels for 198 days and more than 8,700 miles to Hong Kong.

The Printer that Traveled 13,000 Miles

A printer is dropped off in Lawrence, Massachusetts. A GPS tracker has been placed inside the printer. It travels for 131 days and more than 13,000 miles to scrapyard in Hong Kong.

Irvine to Hong Kong

A LCD TV is dropped in Irvine, California. A GPS tracker has been placed inside the TV. It travels for more than 7,000 miles over the course of 270 days to a scrapyard in Hong Kong.
