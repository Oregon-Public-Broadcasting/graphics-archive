The Bureau of Land Management permits livestock grazing on 155 million acres of public land in the West. Those permits come with expanding environmental regulations. But an analysis by EarthFix found enforcement of these rules is lagging.

  • Millions of acres of public rangeland have never been fully assessed for rangeland health.
  • Most of these acres lack long-term management plans and at least one in three permits are renewed under a provision that sidesteps a full environmental review.
  • In hundreds of cases, permits are renewed under that provision even where grazing has been determined to impair rangeland health.
