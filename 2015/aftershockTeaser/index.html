Aftershock

Coming May 8th

What is Aftershock?

Aftershock creates a personalized story for Oregonians about the risks of the Cascadia earthquake based on the user's location. Each story explains these different risks – shaking, tsunami risk, soil liquefaction – to the reader and how they can prepare for them.

How does it work?

Personalized reports are driven by data modeled by the Oregon Department of Geology and Mineral Industries. Geographic data sets of Oregon include: expected shaking, tsunami zone, soil liquefaction PGD, landslide PGD and impact zones determined by the Oregon Resilience Report. All data sets are created with best estimates around the Cascadia Quake. The descriptions of risk and how to prepare for those risks are written based off best estimates from DOGMAI, OSSPAC, The Red Cross and FEMA.

Who made it?

Aftershock is a product of “Storytelling With Data” hosted by The University of Oregon School of Journalism and Communication Agora Journalism Center and Hack Oregon. The team included Andrew Haddock, Weston Wedding and Brandon Stump of Sticky, Dan Logan of Portland State University, Geoff Ostrove of the University of Oregon, and Jason Bernert and Tony Schick of OPB.
