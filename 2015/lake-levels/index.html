Each line represents 1 of 63 monitored reservoirs in Oregon and Washington. You can see how reservoir levels rise above average in wetter summers and drop below average in drier summers.

It was extremely dry this year. Most reservoirs were below their summer average, but nine reached their lowest storage on record this summer.

Some lakes have a lot of natural variation.

Some don't.

Dips like this year's aren't unheard of. The mid-2000s, mid-1990s, and each decade before it showed similar rise and fall across the Northwest.

Drier than before? The past 100 years of data show reservoirs in the Northwest have spiked below average further and with more frequency since 1990.

OPB and EarthFix obtained reservoir storage data for all reservoirs currently tracked by the USDA's Natural Resources Conservation Service, as well as snow-water equivalent (a common snowpack measurement) for the region's snowpack measurement sites and hydrologic basins. Data
